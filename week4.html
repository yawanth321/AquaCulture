Weekly Report

Week-4 (From Dt. 03-06-2024 to Dt. 08-06-2024)

Objective of the Activity Done: Fish farming, profit & loss, problems and schemes of fishermen.

ON DAY 1:
WENT TO FISH FARMING PITS - Fishing farming pits, also known as fish ponds or fish farms, are specialized areas designed for the cultivation and rearing of fish. These pits are man-made or natural depressions in the ground that are specifically designed to accommodate fish populations. Visiting fish farming pits provides insights into the operations, techniques, and challenges associated with fish farming, including location selection, construction, stocking, feeding, water quality management, disease control, and harvesting.
ON DAY 2:
KNOWN ABOUT BREEDING OF FISHES - Breeding of fish in specially constructed tanks and ponds is known as Pisciculture. Fish breeding involves various methods, including induced breeding through the use of hormones. Fish breeding season typically starts in March and continues up to August in rivers. Understanding fish breeding provides insights into the complexities and strategies involved in maintaining fish populations for commercial or recreational purposes.
ON DAY 3:
LOSS AND PROFIT OF FISH MARKETING - Fish marketing profitability depends on factors such as production costs, market demand, pricing strategies, value-added products, marketing channels, quality control, and branding. Analyzing these aspects helps in understanding the financial dynamics of fish marketing and optimizing business strategies to achieve profitability amidst market uncertainties.
ON DAY 4:
CHECKED THE FRESHNESS OF THE FISHES - Ensuring fish freshness involves sensory checks such as smelling for mild scent, inspecting eyes for clarity, checking texture for firmness, observing appearance for freshness indicators, and examining gills for bright color. These checks are essential for maintaining quality and ensuring consumer satisfaction in fish markets.
ON DAY 5:
KNOWN THE PROBLEMS OF FISHERMEN - Fishermen face challenges such as overfishing, declining fish stocks, market competition, environmental hazards, access to fishing grounds, lack of infrastructure, regulatory issues, and safety concerns at sea. Addressing these challenges requires sustainable fishing practices, effective fisheries management, and support for fishing communities to ensure long-term sustainability and livelihoods.
ON DAY 6:
PROPAGATED ABOUT FISH SCHEMES & LOANS - Various schemes and loans support fishermen, including subsidies, grants, loans for fishing equipment, working capital, aqua farming, insurance, market support, infrastructure, capacity building, and subsidized interest rates. Schemes like the YSR Matsyakara Bharosa provide financial aid and benefits to fishermen, enhancing their livelihoods and promoting sustainable fishing practices.
