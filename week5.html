Weekly Report

Week-5 (From Dt. 10-06-2024 to Dt. 15-06-2024)

Objective of the Activity Done: AQUACULTURE PLANTS & HYDROPONICS PLANTS

ON DAY 1:
KNOWN ABOUT THE AQUACULTURE PLANTS - Aquaculture involves the controlled cultivation of aquatic plants and animals using wastewater as a nutrient source. Aquaculture plant production utilizes methods like hydroponics and floating plant ponds, with advantages such as rapid growth, job creation, low capital costs, and continuous cultivation. However, challenges include space requirements and potential invasiveness of some plants.
ON DAY 2:
AQUATIC PLANTS & HYDROPONIC VEGES - Aquatic plants thrive in water environments like lakes and ponds, providing oxygen, food, and shelter. Hydroponic vegetable plants, grown without soil in nutrient-rich water solutions, include lettuce, spinach, tomatoes, and herbs. Hydroponics offers controlled, space-efficient agriculture compared to traditional methods.
ON DAY 3:
KNOWN ABOUT HYDROPONICS - Hydroponics enables plant growth without soil, using nutrient-rich water solutions. Advantages include resource efficiency, faster growth rates, year-round cultivation, space savings, and versatility in various settings. Hydroponics is ideal for urban farming and sustainable food production.
ON DAY 4:
LEARN THE HYDROPONICS SETUP STEPS - Setting up a hydroponic system involves using suitable containers, drilling holes for plant placement, assembling an air pump, preparing nutrient solutions, adjusting pH levels, and starting the pump. Hydroponics allows for flexible and profitable farming in various environmental conditions.
ON DAY 5:
ADVERTISED ON HYDROPONICS PLANTS - Hydroponics promotes increased yield, water efficiency, space savings, year-round cultivation, reduced pesticide use, and improved crop quality. By highlighting these benefits, people are encouraged to explore hydroponics for sustainable and efficient plant cultivation.
ON DAY 6:
EXPLAINED ABOUT USES OF HYDROPONIC PLANTS - Hydroponic plants serve diverse purposes such as food production, urban farming, water conservation, research, education, ornamental cultivation, and therapeutic gardens. Educating others on hydroponics inspires interest and excitement in growing plants hydroponically.
